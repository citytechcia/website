About CIA


  • What?

    The Computer Information Association (CIA) is a club for CST students by CST students. They work hard to make sure that students are as dedicated and committed to their studies in the classroom as they are out of the classroom.

  • Where?

    The club is located in the Namm Building of the New York City College of Technology on the 9th floor in room N905. Club Hours run from 1 – 2 pm on Thursday.

  • Why?

    A lot of students feel like the classroom isn’t enough to prepare them for the working world. We want to give the dedicated and hardworking students of the school opportunities to strengthen their skills and provide them with the confidence they need.

  • How?

    The officers of the club set up and host special events to inspire and test club members. This can range from guest speakers to contest and even a mock interview. In addition to this, all club members are free to spread the word on any other opportunities they find.

Our Goal! & Perception

The Computer Club is a student organization that provides a creative and safe learning environment where college students interested in computers and technology come together to explore their own ideas. The club will plan and organize projects and activities, outside the classroom, with the purpose of enhancing technical skills and knowledge. It is designed to create an environment of team work, along with, enhancing interpersonal and social skills.
